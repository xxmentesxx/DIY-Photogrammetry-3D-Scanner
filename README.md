📸 DIY Photogrammetry Station (Scrap-Built 3D Scanner)An automated, IoT-based 3D scanning solution built with recycled scanner parts and 3D printed components. This station uses an ESP32-S3 and Python to synchronize a turntable and a vertical elevator for high-quality photogrammetry.✨ FeaturesFully Automated: Synchronized movement between the turntable (rotation) and elevator (Z-axis).IoT Control: Web-based interface to monitor and control the scan from any device.Smartphone Integration: Uses your phone's camera via a browser-based stream, saving photos directly to your PC.High-Torque Drive: Custom firmware logic to get maximum power from small 28BYJ-48 stepper motors.Smart Cropping: Real-time "Crop Box" selection to save only the subject, reducing background noise and disk space.🛠 Hardware & Recycling (BOM)This project is a "Scrap-Build," meaning it's designed to use parts from old office equipment.Recycled Parts: 2x Linear rods and bushings (salvaged from an old paper scanner).Z-Axis Drive: Continuous timing belt system (e.g., 380MXL) with 3D printed gear reduction.Motors: 2x 28BYJ-48 Stepper Motors with ULN2003 drivers.Tilt Control: 1x MG996R Servo motor for camera angle adjustment.Brain: ESP32-S3 (or standard ESP32).Power: External 5V 3A DC power supply (Mandatory: Do not power from ESP32).Note on 3D Printing: Since linear rods vary between different scanner models, you may need to adjust the hole diameters in your slicer (using "Horizontal Expansion") to fit your specific rods.🔌 Wiring Guide (Pinout)ComponentConnectionESP32 PinTurntable MotorIN1, IN2, IN3, IN4Pins 8, 9, 10, 11Elevator MotorIN1, IN2, IN3, IN4Pins 6, 5, 7, 4Servo MotorSignal (Orange/Yellow)Pin 3PowerGND (Common Ground)GND🚀 Installation & Setup1.
